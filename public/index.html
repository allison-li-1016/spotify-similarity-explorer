Artists are being sued over similar-sounding songs

In the age of TikTok songs and democratization of music production, some big artists are being sued for similar-sounding songs. Many of these cases have been heavily documented in popular media and artists popularity has also contributed to bigger artists generally facing more criticism for creating songs that sound similar to ones created by less popular artists. Some notable examples are listed below. Notable instances include:

  • Dua Lipa gets accused of plagiarism for her song "Levitating"
  • Olivia Rodrigo's bridge in "Deja Vu" resembles Taylor Swift's bridge in "Cruel Summer"
  • Sam Smith faces lawsuit for song "Stay With Me"

    • What makes songs sound similar to each other?

    There are many criteria that influence songs. In the example above, see how songs like California Gurls and Tik TikTok have much higher rates for danceability and energy compared to other songs such as "Stay With Me" and "I Won't Back Down" (as noted by the different range values). Some features of similar-sounding songs include Danceability, Energy, Tempo and Time Signature (Rhythm). A less measurable feature includes the lyrics themselves, such as in the case of Dua Lipa's song "Levitating". In this case, the lyrics rather than other quantitative measures of the song were noted as very similar. All these factors contribute to making a song recognizable. To demonstrate the similarity between various songs, you can select song criteria below to see similarities between Spotify's top 500 most popular songs released in the last 100 years, based on the Spotify popularity index, which is a measure calculated using a Spotify song's total streams. You can also change the range of various song criteria to see which songs fall into that numerical range for Spotify's danceability, energy, tempo and time signature calculated measures. In addition, you can check and uncheck specific songs you want to include int he visualization, as well as search for songs you wish to include.
