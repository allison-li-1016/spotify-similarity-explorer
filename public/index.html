Spotify Similarity Explorer for Top Tracks

Team Members: Arya GJ; Emily Chang; Duaa Zaheer; Allison Li

Our project explores the Spotify track dataset, which highlights audio features of over 600k tracks created over the last 100 years. It uses popularity metrics such as number of interactions and streams to rank the most popular Spotify songs and artists. In this visualization, you can view the popularity index of the top 200 Spotify artists, check and uncheck artists you want to include in the graph, search for artists and edit the popularity index range to see which artists fall into what specific popularity index ranges.

Artists are being sued over similar-sounding songs

In the age of TikTok songs and democratization of music production, some big artists are being sued for similar-sounding songs. Many of these cases have been heavily documented in popular media and artists popularity has also contributed to bigger artists generally facing more criticism for creating songs that sound similar to ones created by less popular artists. Some notable examples are listed below. Notable instances include:

  • Dua Lipa gets accused of plagiarism for her song "Levitating"
  • Olivia Rodrigo's bridge in "Deja Vu" resembles Taylor Swift's bridge in "Cruel Summer"
  • Sam Smith faces lawsuit for song "Stay With Me"

    • What makes songs sound similar to each other?

    There are many criteria that influence songs. In the example above, see how songs like California Gurls and Tik TikTok have much higher rates for danceability and energy compared to other songs such as "Stay With Me" and "I Won't Back Down" (as noted by the different range values). We also took a look at Olivia Rodrigo's Deja Vu vs. Taylor Swift's Cruel Summer given the controversy regarding the similarity of the bridge. Some features of similar-sounding songs include Danceability, Energy, Tempo and Time Signature (Rhythm). A less measurable feature includes the lyrics themselves, such as in the case of Dua Lipa's song "Levitating". In this case, the lyrics rather than other quantitative measures of the song were noted as very similar. All these factors contribute to making a song recognizable. To demonstrate the similarity between various songs, you can select song criteria below to see similarities between Spotify's top 500 most popular songs released in the last 100 years, based on the Spotify popularity index, which is a measure calculated using a Spotify song's total streams. You can also change the range of various song criteria to see which songs fall into that numerical range for Spotify's danceability, energy, tempo and time signature calculated measures. In addition, you can check and uncheck specific songs you want to include int he visualization, as well as search for songs you wish to include.

    How have popular songs changed over time?

    Trends come and go, as do music tastes. In the visualization below, see how the average features of popular songs have changed over time in the last 100 years. What do listeners value more in songs? Do they value danceability? Tempo? Do certain time signatures give off better vibes than others? In this visualization, you can look at average metrics (danceability, tempo, etc.) over time for the top 5 songs of that year by popularity index. A major drop in the danceability and energy of songs can be seen in the period of time preceding the Great Depression, as well as a peak during the Roaring 20s. The general danceability and energy of popular songs has also been steadily increasing over time as the data approaches modern day pop music.
